Incremental annotation updates

We fully support incremental updates - we update to new WormBase genomes frequently. Small updates are fine, e.g. we’ve updated about a hundred S. mansoni genes in WBPS12.

This doesn’t happen much for outside genomes.

I would be happy to see annotation updates of draft genomes driven by more data (e.g. RNASeq) and extra manual attention, but this is not really happening, apart from:

  • A. ceylanicum, posted to WashU website
  • M. corti, by Pepe’s student Alicia
  • Ascaris update by UC Denver after they botched their first attempt

Other data types

Omics data is challenging for us to provide.

  • the information is per gene / DNA region, and how data was collected from the organism
  • heterogeneity of formats and data types
  • very uneven availability for our parasites

Each kind of data arguably warrants its own resource, and ideally some EBI resource would sort us out for it. For RNASeq, it didn’t work out, and we decided to implement our own solution.

Proteomics

There are papers out there for S. mansoni and S. japonicum. We currently offer no support for these. EBI’s proteomics resource - PRIDE - hosts them, but it is primarily an archive.

Names that our users use

Fitzsimmons et al (2012) have found a family of 13 tegument allergen-like genes, SMTAL{1..13}. We would like to make sure people can find it, but:

  • functional descriptions come from UniProt and RefSeq, some of which is curated to include references like this one
  • this particular entry was stored as synonym
  • we rely on Ensembl’s parser of UniProt data, that doesn’t include what we needed, and it’s hard to extend
  • UniProt doesn’t have a process for importing genomes when they’re not submitted to archives

We’re addressing this, but it’s quite difficult.

UniProt has imported S.mansoni v7 now (available WBPS14) and took in a few more annotations e.g. new Haemonchus (should be available WBPS15) but no there is no reliable process for this. We’ll try notifying them after our releases.

We’ll try writing a custom parser and import more of UniProt’s curation, but it may not be very general.

No plans to provide our own functional descriptions yet - I think it would be difficult to get right.
